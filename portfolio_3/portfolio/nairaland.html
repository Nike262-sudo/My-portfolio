Nairaland
Redesign

Improving usability and readability for a legacy forum platform

A redesign of Nairaland focused on improving readability, navigation, and overall user experience while maintaining its core functionality.

Role
Product Designer
Type
UX/UI Redesign
Timeline
1–2 weeks

Nairaland Redesign — full interface overview

01 — Overview

The platform

Nairaland is one of the largest online forums in Nigeria, but its interface has remained largely unchanged over the years. This project explores how a modern redesign could improve usability and readability without losing what makes the platform familiar.

02 — Problem

What needed to change

Nairaland carries a significant amount of content per page — long threads, dense post lists, and category navigation all competing for attention at once. Over time, these patterns have become barriers to comfortable use.

  • 01 Text is displayed at small sizes with minimal line height, making extended reading uncomfortable and tiring — a critical issue for a platform where users spend long periods reading through threads.
  • 02 Thread lists lack visual separation — post titles, metadata, and reply counts are compressed together with no clear spacing, making it hard to scan and identify relevant threads quickly.
  • 03 No clear visual hierarchy between page sections means users must actively search for context rather than having it surface naturally through layout
  • 04 Navigation labels are ambiguous and inconsistently placed — new users often struggle to understand how sections are organized or how to move between them without trial and error.

Individually, each of these is a minor irritation. Together, they create a browsing experience that demands more from users than it should — leading to disengagement before they find what they came for.

03 — Goal

Design objectives

The goal was to redesign the platform in a way that modernizes the experience without disrupting the familiarity users already have with it.

  • —Improve readability and content structure throughout the interface
  • —Create a clearer visual hierarchy to help users scan and navigate content
  • —Simplify navigation to reduce friction for both new and returning users
  • —Modernize the interface while preserving the familiarity of the original
04 — Design Approach

Balancing modernization with familiarity

The redesign focused on making targeted, meaningful improvements — not reinventing the product from scratch.

1
Improved Hierarchy
Forums are inherently layered — categories contain boards, boards contain threads, threads contain posts. When that structure isn't reflected visually, users are forced to reconstruct it mentally on every page.
2
Better Readability
On a content-heavy platform like Nairaland, readability isn't a nice-to-have — it directly affects how long users stay and how much they read. Increasing font size, line height, and contrast was the highest-priority fix.
3
Simplified Navigation
When users can't predict where a link will take them, they stop exploring. Reorganizing the navigation around actual user tasks — browsing by category, finding active threads, returning to recent content — restores that predictability.
4
Modern UI
Nairaland has millions of regular users with deeply ingrained habits. The risk of a full visual overhaul is alienating that base. The approach was to modernize the interface incrementally — improving structure and clarity without discarding what users recognize.
05 — Before vs After

The difference

A direct comparison between the original interface and the redesign — showing what changed and why each decision was made.

Before
Thread titles, post counts, and category labels are packed together with no breathing room. The lack of spacing forces users to read every line rather than scan — significantly slowing navigation.
After
Consistent padding between thread rows, a clear typographic hierarchy, and visible separation between categories allow users to scan the page in seconds and go directly to what they need.
06 — Key Improvements

What changed

Each improvement was driven by a specific usability issue identified in the original interface.

Typography & Readability
Base font size was increased and line height set to 1.7 to make reading longer threads comfortable. A structured type scale — with clear distinctions between headings, thread titles, and metadata — helps users parse content at a glance.
Layout Structure
Consistent vertical spacing was added between thread rows, and section headers were made visually distinct from content. This separation gives users a mental model of the page before they begin reading.
Navigation
Navigation items were reorganized around the tasks users actually perform: browsing categories, finding active threads, and returning to recent activity. Labels were rewritten for clarity, and the overall structure was simplified.
Visual Design
Decoration was removed in favor of structure. Subtle 1px dividers replace heavy borders. A neutral off-white background reduces contrast fatigue during long reading sessions.

07 — Visual Design

Design decisions

The visual direction focuses on clarity and usability — making every design choice serve the content rather than compete with it.

Typography Clean, legible typefaces — optimized for long-form reading
Spacing system Consistent scale that separates content clearly without wasting space
Color usage Minimal — black, white, and grey only for maximum clarity
Contrast Improved contrast ratios to support readability across all content types
08 — Outcome

What was achieved

The redesigned interface directly addresses the friction points identified in the original — making everyday browsing faster, less effortful, and more predictable.

  • —Faster content scanning — users can identify relevant threads and categories without reading every line on the page
  • —Improved readability — increased font size, line height, and spacing make extended reading sessions significantly more comfortable.
  • —More structured browsing — clearer navigation and consistent layout patterns reduce the cognitive effort required to move through the platform.

The result is an interface that feels immediately more usable to new users, while remaining familiar enough that existing users experience it as an upgrade — not a replacement.

09 — Reflection

Looking back

Redesigning an existing product with a large, loyal user base is a different challenge from designing from scratch. The goal isn't to express a design vision — it's to solve real friction without introducing new confusion.

Key takeaways

  • Designing for existing users means earning their trust incrementally — not resetting their expectations all at once
  • Familiarity is a design asset. Preserving recognizable patterns while improving the details is harder than redesigning freely, but produces better outcomes for real users
  • On content-heavy platforms, spacing and typography do more UX work than any visual feature

What I'd improve

  • Test with actual Nairaland users — assumptions about scanning behavior need validation before they become design decisions
  • Design the mobile experience in parallel — the majority of Nigerian internet users are on mobile, making it the primary surface, not a secondary one
  • Avoid adding complexity in the name of modernity — every new element should solve a specific usability problem, not just look cleaner
