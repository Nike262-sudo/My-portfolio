Educraft

Designing a modern e-learning experience

A platform designed to simplify how users discover and enroll in practical, career-focused courses.

Product Designer
Tools

Timeline

Educraft — Hero section overview

01 — Overview

What is Educraft?

Educraft is an e-learning platform designed to help users discover and enroll in practical, career-focused courses. The goal was to create a structured and conversion-focused experience that simplifies the learning journey.

02 — Problem

What was broken?

Many e-learning platforms overwhelm users with too many options and poor content structure, making it difficult to quickly identify relevant courses.

  • 01 Understand what the platform offers at a glance
  • 02 Identify courses that match their goals or skill level
  • 03 Trust the platform enough to take action

This creates friction between discovery and enrollment, leading to drop-offs before users engage with the product.

User pain point mapping

Competitor audit — key gaps

03 — Goal

Design objectives

The goal of this project was to design a clear and structured experience that:

◎
Simplify Discovery
Simplifies how users discover and identify courses relevant to their goals.
◈
Improve Clarity
Improves content hierarchy and readability across all key sections.
◇
Build Trust
Builds trust through well-organized information and credibility signals.
▷
Drive Enrollment
Encourages users to take action — enroll or sign up — at the right moments.
04 — Design Approach

How I approached the design

1
Visual Hierarchy
Content was structured using strong typography and spacing to guide users through the page naturally. Each section has a clear focal point — nothing competes for attention.
2
Minimalism
Unnecessary elements were removed to reduce cognitive load and improve focus. White space was used intentionally to create clarity between sections.
3
Conversion Flow
Each section was intentionally designed to move users closer to enrolling — from discovery to trust to decision. Structure follows strategy throughout the experience.

Early wireframe explorations — structure before style

05 — Key Sections

Building the experience

Each section of the landing page was designed intentionally. Here's the rationale behind the five most critical parts of the layout.

Hero Section
The hero section was designed to immediately communicate the platform's value with a clear headline and a focused call-to-action. The layout prioritizes clarity, not decoration.
Course Section
Courses are presented in a clean card-based layout, making it easy for users to scan and compare options. Spacing and alignment were carefully used to improve readability.
Testimonials
A testimonial section was introduced to build credibility and reduce hesitation, helping users feel more confident about enrolling.
Pricing
Pricing was structured to allow easy comparison between options, helping users quickly understand value and choose a plan without confusion.
Call-to-Action
Call-to-action elements were placed at key points throughout the page to capture users at different stages of decision-making.

06 — Visual Design

Design decisions

The visual design focuses on clarity and consistency across the entire interface.

Typography Clean typefaces chosen to improve readability and hierarchy
Spacing system Consistent spacing scale to create structure and visual rhythm
Aesthetic Minimal — unnecessary elements removed to reduce distractions
Color Subtle contrast used intentionally to guide user attention
07 — Implementation

From design to live product

The design was translated into a live, responsive interface. Tools like Claude AI were used to accelerate development and ensure consistency between design and implementation, allowing for rapid iteration.

09 — Reflection

Looking back

This project reinforced the importance of designing for clarity and user decision-making.

Key takeaways

  • Structure and hierarchy are critical in content-heavy interfaces
  • Simplicity improves usability and overall engagement
  • Designing with intent leads to better user outcomes

What I'd improve

  • Conduct user testing to validate layout and content decisions
  • Explore personalization features to improve course relevance
  • Expand the platform into a more complete product experience beyond the landing page
