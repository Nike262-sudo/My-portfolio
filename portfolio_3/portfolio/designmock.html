Project Overview

Practicing interviews should feel like interviewing with a real hiring team.

DesignMock AI was created to bridge the gap between studying UX theory and performing confidently in real product design interviews. Instead of static interview questions, designers interact with an AI interviewer that evaluates their thinking, communication, and product reasoning before delivering structured feedback that helps them improve with every session.

The Challenge

Most designers prepare alone.

After speaking with aspiring product designers and reflecting on my own interview preparation, I noticed a recurring pattern. Most people relied on YouTube videos, blog articles, or random mock questions. While these resources were helpful, they rarely recreated the pressure and unpredictability of an actual product design interview. There was no structured way to practice communicating decisions, defending trade-offs, or receiving meaningful feedback.

01
No realistic interview simulation.
02
Generic feedback with no actionable direction.
03
No way to measure improvement over time.
Research

Understanding how designers prepare today.

Rather than designing another collection of interview questions, I wanted to understand how designers currently prepare for interviews. Most participants described preparation as inconsistent. Some rehearsed with friends. Others watched interview recordings repeatedly. Many admitted they had no idea whether their answers were actually good until a real interview.

The biggest insight wasn't that designers lacked information. They lacked feedback. Learning happens through iteration, and interviews provide very few opportunities to practice before the real thing. That became the central opportunity behind DesignMock AI.

Design Principles

Three principles guided every design decision.

01

Feel conversational.

The interface should reduce anxiety and encourage thoughtful discussion rather than feeling like an examination.

02

Provide meaningful feedback.

Every interview should end with actionable recommendations that designers can immediately apply.

03

Track progress over time.

Instead of isolated interview sessions, the experience should motivate continuous improvement through measurable progress.

Experience Flow

From opening the app to becoming interview-ready.

Product Walkthrough

A guided interview experience that feels natural.

The experience begins by allowing designers to choose the type of interview they want to practice. Whether preparing for a portfolio presentation, a product thinking interview, or a whiteboard challenge, every session adapts to the selected format. Rather than displaying a list of static questions, the AI behaves like an interviewer, asking follow-up questions and encouraging deeper explanations.

Feature One

Real conversations instead of scripted questionnaires.

Every interview unfolds dynamically. The AI adapts to previous answers, probes for reasoning, and simulates the back-and-forth conversations designers experience during actual interviews. This creates a more realistic environment where communication skills become just as important as technical knowledge.

Feature Two

Detailed AI feedback after every session.

Once an interview is completed, DesignMock AI generates structured feedback covering communication, product thinking, confidence, problem-solving, storytelling, and decision-making. Instead of generic encouragement, designers receive specific recommendations they can apply before the next interview.

Feature Three

Track improvement over time.

Preparing for interviews is an iterative process. Every completed interview contributes to a broader understanding of progress, helping designers identify recurring weaknesses while celebrating measurable improvement. Progress dashboards reinforce consistency and encourage deliberate practice.

Final Interface
Design System

Building consistency across the experience.

A simple design language was created to keep every interaction familiar and distraction-free. Typography emphasizes readability, spacing creates breathing room, and a restrained color palette allows users to focus entirely on interview conversations and feedback.

Responsive Experience

Designed for desktop, tablet and mobile.

Outcome

Helping designers practice with confidence.

DesignMock AI transforms interview preparation from passive learning into active practice. By combining conversational AI with personalized coaching, designers gain a safe environment to improve communication, sharpen product thinking, and build confidence before meeting real hiring teams.

Beyond solving an immediate problem, the project demonstrates how AI can become a practical learning companion rather than simply another content generator.
