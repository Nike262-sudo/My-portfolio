Project Overview

Practicing interviews should feel like interviewing with a real hiring team.

DesignMock AI bridges the gap between studying UX theory and performing confidently during real product design interviews. Instead of relying on static interview questions, designers interact with an AI interviewer that evaluates their thinking, communication, and product reasoning before generating structured feedback after every session.

The Challenge

Most designers prepare alone.

After speaking with aspiring product designers and reflecting on my own interview preparation, I noticed a recurring pattern. Most designers relied on YouTube videos, blog posts, or random interview questions scattered across the internet.

While these resources were helpful, they rarely recreated the pressure, unpredictability, and communication required during an actual product design interview.

There was no structured way to practice explaining decisions, defending trade-offs, or receiving meaningful feedback after each session.

01

03
No way to track improvement across multiple interviews.
Research

Understanding how designers prepare today.

Rather than designing another collection of interview questions, I wanted to understand how designers currently prepare for interviews.

Most participants described preparation as inconsistent. Some practiced with friends, while others repeatedly watched interview recordings or searched through articles hoping to find useful questions.

Many admitted they had no idea whether their answers were actually good until they were already inside a real interview.

The biggest insight wasn't that designers lacked information—they lacked feedback. Interviews improve through repetition, but there are very few opportunities to practice before the real thing.

That became the core opportunity behind DesignMock AI.

Design Principles

Three principles guided every design decision.

01

Feel conversational.

The interface should reduce anxiety and feel like a genuine conversation rather than a formal examination.

02

Provide meaningful feedback.

Every interview should end with actionable recommendations designers can immediately apply before their next session.

03

Track progress over time.

Instead of isolated practice sessions, the product should encourage continuous improvement through measurable progress and consistent reflection.

Experience Flow

From opening the app to becoming interview-ready.

Product Walkthrough

A guided interview experience that feels natural.

The experience begins by allowing designers to choose the type of interview they want to practice. Whether preparing for portfolio presentations, product thinking interviews, or whiteboard challenges, every session adapts to the selected format.

Instead of displaying a list of static questions, the AI behaves like an interviewer—asking follow-up questions, challenging assumptions, and encouraging deeper explanations.

Feature One

Real conversations instead of scripted questionnaires.

Every interview unfolds dynamically. The AI adapts to previous answers, probes for reasoning, and simulates the same back-and-forth discussions designers experience during real interviews.

This creates a realistic environment where communication skills become just as important as technical knowledge.

Feature Two

Detailed AI feedback after every interview.

After every interview session, DesignMock AI generates structured feedback covering communication, storytelling, confidence, product thinking, problem solving, and decision-making.

Instead of generic encouragement, designers receive practical recommendations they can immediately apply before their next interview.

Feature Three

Track improvement over time.

Preparing for interviews is an iterative process. Every completed interview contributes to a broader understanding of progress, helping designers identify recurring weaknesses while celebrating measurable improvement.

Progress dashboards encourage consistency and transform interview preparation into an ongoing learning journey instead of a one-time event.

Reflection

What I learned.

This project reinforced the importance of designing experiences instead of simply designing interfaces. Product design interviews are fundamentally conversations, so the product itself needed to encourage thoughtful dialogue rather than presenting users with another list of questions.

I also learned how valuable structured feedback can be. Designers improve fastest when they understand not only what went wrong, but also why it happened and how to improve before the next interview.

DesignMock AI became an exploration of confidence, communication, and continuous learning—not just interview preparation.
